Swami Vivekananda

1863-1902

Maker of Modern India

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was an Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, and social reformer. He was a chief disciple of the 19th-century mystic Ramakrishna and a key figure in the introduction of Vedanta and Yoga to the West. He is often called the "Messenger of Indian Wisdom" or the "Maker of Modern India". Vivekananda was born Narendranath Datta in Calcutta, India. He was a brilliant student and a natural leader. He was also deeply interested in spirituality, and he began to study the Hindu scriptures at a young age. In 1881, Vivekananda met Ramakrishna, a Hindu mystic who became his guru. Ramakrishna taught Vivekananda about the importance of devotion, service, and renunciation.

Important Works

  • He introduced Hinduism to the Western world. In 1893, Swami Vivekananda gave a speech at the World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago, which helped to introduce Hinduism to a global audience. His speech was a major turning point in the history of Hinduism, and it helped to raise awareness of Indian culture and spirituality around the world.
  • He inspired the Indian independence movement. Swami Vivekananda's speeches and writings about the greatness of India's past and the potential of its people helped to inspire the Indian independence movement. He was a strong advocate for social reform and national unity, and he believed that India could only achieve independence if it first achieved spiritual and moral regeneration.
  • He promoted social reform. Swami Vivekananda was a strong advocate for social reform, and he spoke out against social ills such as caste discrimination, poverty, and illiteracy. He founded the Ramakrishna Mission, which is a major social service organization that works to improve the lives of people in India.
  • He emphasized the importance of education. Swami Vivekananda believed that education was essential for India's progress, and he worked to promote education for all sections of society. He founded several educational institutions, including the Ramakrishna Mission Vivekananda University.
  • He inspired the youth of India. Swami Vivekananda was a great inspiration to the youth of India, and he urged them to take up the challenge of nation-building. He believed that the youth were the future of India, and he called on them to strive for excellence in all spheres of life
